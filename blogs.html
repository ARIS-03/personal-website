    • What's the feeling after getting into a Tier-3 college

      August, 2021

      This blog is gonna interesting because everyone wants to talk about their good experiences but this experience is a little bit different its a mixture of some good & bad experiences which many students go through in there life when they dream to get into top institutes of India (IIT's) but some of them make it possible and some of us don't. The journey starts from 9 class where we were told by our parents that we want to become doctors and engineers and want to take admission to top colleges. But after doing so much hard work we get into Tier-3 colleges but the pain doesn't stop here because now we have to face some kind of discrimination because we didn't perform well in our JEE exam so now it's time to pay off. The feeling we get is that we get a good job after our BTech degree is completed. We can get into big MNC's make our parents proud after they spend lakh's of money on our education or we have to sell fruits and vegetables on roadside. This kind of fear comes when we get into a tier 3 college but I come over this fear when I see that it can do hard work anything is possible.

      But someone of says if you are that much a hardworking guy then the way you not too into IIT's so I want to say that in India the career starts from the age of 25 after completing higher education and what does thing means its mean at a very young age we totally depend on our parents we think ourselves we are so much hardworking which is not true. Before this, our hard work is shown up by our grades and we are busy in coaching institutes and this thing is not hard work where we are depended on our parents at a very young age its's not fault our parents the surrounding which we are in is like that so we cannot do anything. If our surrounding is not enabling us to do better in life, so it's better to change the surrounding but at that time the surrounding was we get into IIT.Try to think 10 lakh students sit for a particular exam and is guaranteed 1% of students get into IIT's and some other good colleges so what about the 99% who not into these colleges so what does it mean they are useless this kind of discrimination doesn't stop here you see some recruiters what they write only tier-1 fresher or graduates so what about 95% and doesn't mean that all are useless everyone done some hard-work and acquire some skill. I want to conclude that if you are capable to get into IIT or some good colleges it's well and good to go for it but don't think if u not get into a top college everything is over but at the end of the day your hard work will get you a better life.
